## Understanding Your Documentation Needs

Participating in a Haitian election typically requires proof of Haitian citizenship. The specific documents required can vary depending on whether you are registering for the first time, updating existing registration, or voting in person or by proxy.

## Common Documents

### Haitian National Identification Card (CIN)

The Carte d'Identification Nationale (CIN) is the primary identification document for Haitian citizens. It is issued by the Office National d'Identification (ONI).

**If you are in the diaspora**, you may need to apply for or renew your CIN through the nearest Haitian consulate.

### Haitian Passport

A valid Haitian passport can serve as proof of citizenship in many contexts. If your passport is expired, contact your nearest Haitian consulate.

### Birth Certificate

A Haitian birth certificate (acte de naissance) is often required to obtain or renew other documents.

## Steps for Diaspora Haitians

1. Locate your nearest Haitian consulate or embassy
2. Determine which documents you currently hold
3. Contact the consulate to understand the renewal or application process
4. Begin the process as early as possible, as processing times can vary

## Important Note

This guide is for informational purposes only. Official requirements are set by Haitian government institutions. Always verify current requirements with the relevant authority.
